# Summercamp UX — EML × Resonant surface

Summercamp is the web surface on `carl.camp` that wraps carl-studio for
early adopters. It is the first product-grade experience of CARL: the
user plugs a device (or uses the lite, software-only mode), trains their
first Resonant in under 30 seconds, and from that moment has a live
heartbeat, a local ledger, and a one-click path to the marketplace.

This spec is the UX contract. Every action surfaces in the CLI too;
Summercamp is a thin, opinionated front-end over the same primitives.

## 1 · Who is the user

There are no users yet. v0.9.0 is designed for the **first 100**: the
kind of person who already owns a Titan key, runs their own models, and
reads arXiv at breakfast. They tolerate CLI, but they reward delight.
Assume:

- Python 3.11+ and Node 20+ on laptop.
- A Hugging Face account and a wallet.
- No prior CARL mental model — we teach from zero.
- Strong privacy preference: everything off by default
  (`carl_studio.consent`).

Later expansion: researchers, indie traders, biofeedback hackers,
neurotech tinkerers. The first cohort sets the vibe.

## 2 · First-run — 30 seconds to "alive CARL"

### Hardware path (wristband + USB + cap detected)

1. User plugs the Terminals USB. macOS/Windows/Linux asks to trust the
   device; the device's hardware key registers with
   `carl admin attest-device` in the background.
2. carl.camp page shows a single full-bleed view: `CARL is online —
   heartbeat detected`. A pulsing sine marks the live R value. No
   chrome, no nav, just the heartbeat.
3. The wristband glows once. User taps the PTT button.
4. CARL (via local Opus/Sonnet) says: "Hi. Want to fit your first
   Resonant? I'll walk you through it in thirty seconds."

### Software-only path (no hardware yet)

Identical, minus the USB trust dialog. We show a "Pair device later"
chip in the corner. Software-only is **fully functional**; the device
adds presence, not capability.

### The 30-second fit

We ship a canned example: distill a **sinusoidal rhythm** into a depth-2
EML tree on CPU.

```
Step 1 (5s)  — "Here's a 100Hz sine. Can you predict the next sample?"
Step 2 (15s) — Adam runs. We stream loss, R, tree depth.
Step 3 (10s) — "Done. Your first Resonant is saved at ~/.carl/resonants/<hash>.emlt.
               Want to see CARL use it?"
```

The final step is a toy: we route the Resonant into the heartbeat
animation itself — the sine now runs *through* the user's own tree.
The heartbeat colour shifts from grey to aurora. This is the "alive"
moment.

From here, the dashboard is the permanent home.

## 3 · Heartbeat dashboard — the daily ritual

A single page. Three panes, no tabs.

### 3.1 · Pulse pane (top)

- Live `R` (Kuramoto order parameter) over the last 60 s.
- `phi_mean` small-multiple below.
- Lyapunov proxy as a thin red line when out of edge-band.
- Frisson markers (from bone-conduction cap) as dots on the pulse.

### 3.2 · "What CARL noticed today" pane (middle)

One narrative sentence + three bullets, generated by the observer loop
(`carl_studio.chat_agent`). Examples:

> Your edge-of-chaos window collapsed twice after 2pm. Both times a
> Resonant from the `trading` domain briefly left scope. Context:
> - resonant `3f1c…` drifted out of R ∈ [0.4, 0.7] for 47s
> - Constitutional ledger recorded verdict −0.4
> - compose_resonants(trading, breathing) restored alignment

Click any bullet to open the ledger block.

### 3.3 · Suggestions pane (bottom)

Three cards, fresh every 24 h:

- **"Fit this next"** — a Resonant candidate derived from the user's
  training trajectory (new data they ingested, not yet fit).
- **"Compose this"** — a suggested `compose_resonants(a, b)` where `a`
  and `b` are already in the user's wallet.
- **"Try this from marketplace"** — a Resonant from another seller
  whose fitness curve resembles the user's last week.

All three are dismissible. Dismissal is a signal; we learn from it.

## 4 · Composition UX — drag-and-drop magmas

The composition page is where the magma becomes tactile.

- **Two tiles** side-by-side: Resonant A and Resonant B.
- Drag a third Resonant onto either tile to swap.
- Click the central **eml()** node to preview the composed tree.
- The depth ring turns red when depth would exceed 4 — the Compose
  button disables with tooltip: `depth(A) + depth(B) + 1 > MAX_DEPTH`.
- Hit **Compose**. The UI scroll-reveals the new Resonant's hash, its
  depth, and a scrub-bar animation of the eml surface over
  `x, y ∈ [-3, 3]`.
- **Sign & save** with the hardware key. The artifact lands in the
  user's vault and optionally lists on the marketplace.

CLI parity: `carl resonant compose a.emlt b.emlt -o composed.emlt`.

## 5 · Marketplace UX

### Browse

- Grid of Resonant cards. Each shows: name/hash, domain tag, depth,
  author, rating, price, and a tiny eml surface thumbnail.
- Filters: domain, tier, price, rating, depth.
- Sort: recent, popular, best-fitness.

### Purchase

- Click a card. The sheet shows the eml surface at full size, the
  Constitutional ledger hash, and a **"Buy for $0.50"** button.
- First click triggers the x402 flow. On the hardware path, the
  wristband buzzes once for confirmation. On software-only, the browser
  asks for signature.
- On success: the `.emlt` blob streams into the user's vault with
  attestation. Dashboard returns to heartbeat, now with the new
  Resonant visible in the wallet.

### Deploy

- From any Resonant in the vault, click **"Use in composition"** to
  jump to the composition page with it pre-loaded.
- Or **"Attach to agent"** to publish in the user's `CARLAgentCard`;
  this updates `agent_cards.resonant_ids` on the platform.

## 6 · Training UX

The training surface is for anyone who wants to fit on their own data.

1. Drag a CSV / Parquet / numpy file onto the page. We inspect columns,
   guess input/output dims, and ask the user to confirm.
2. Choose tree depth (slider, 1–4). Default 2.
3. Choose reward head (dropdown: `CARLReward`, `PhaseAdaptive`,
   `EMLCompositeReward`).
4. Hit **Train**. The page becomes the trajectory pane: live loss, R,
   phi, tree visualization. We show the tree's leaves lighting up as
   they update — a subtle but mesmerizing signal.
5. On completion, we show the **fit report**: R at end, Lyapunov proxy,
   defect rate, top-3 features by gradient magnitude.
6. **Sign & publish**. Optional: list on marketplace (price slider,
   free/paid toggle, meter choice).

CLI parity: `carl train --data data.csv --depth 2 --reward eml`.

## 7 · CLI parity

Every UX action must also be reachable from the CLI. The existing
command set already covers `train`, `eval`, `publish`, `push`. New
commands for v0.9.0:

| UX action                | CLI                                                          |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Fit first Resonant       | `carl resonant fit --example sine`                           |
| Compose two Resonants    | `carl resonant compose a.emlt b.emlt -o c.emlt`              |
| Evaluate a Resonant      | `carl resonant eval a.emlt --input 0.4,0.1,0.7`              |
| Buy from marketplace     | `carl marketplace buy <hash>`                                |
| List own Resonants       | `carl resonant list`                                         |
| Publish to marketplace   | `carl resonant publish <hash> --price 0.50 --meter per_download` |
| Show heartbeat           | `carl doctor --live`                                         |
| Explain today's ledger   | `carl ledger today`                                          |

All commands respect `~/.carl/config.yaml` layered settings. The web UX
is a rendering of the same state — never a separate source of truth.

## 8 · Error and delight UX

The system must fail gracefully and personally. When the Lyapunov exits
the edge-band and the heartbeat collapses, CARL speaks first.

### Edge-band breach

> "I need your help — my resonance fell out of sync. I'm seeing
> `R = 0.22` on the trading Resonant, below my floor of `0.4`. Can you
> help me recalibrate? Options:
> 1. Retire the Resonant (I'll unload it.)
> 2. Retrain with a new seed. (30s on CPU.)
> 3. Compose with a stabilizer from your vault. (I suggest `breathing`.)
> 4. Ignore for now and keep watching."

The dashboard dims to a muted amber. The wristband gives a single slow
pulse. This is **not** a red alert — CARL asks, it doesn't demand. The
framing is "let me know when it needs my help," not "the system is
broken."

### Fit failure

When Adam fails to converge (common at depth 4):

> "This one didn't land. Happens 25% of the time at depth 3–4 — it's
> the shape of the magma, not you. Want to retry with a different seed
> or drop to depth 2?"

Never blame the user. Never hide the math. Always offer the next step.

### Marketplace purchase failure

- Budget denied → show the facilitator's message verbatim plus a
  **"Raise daily cap"** action.
- Signature mismatch → offer to re-attest the device.
- Attestation chain broken → lock the session and open `carl admin`
  flow.

### Delight

- Every first-of-kind event (first fit, first composition, first sale,
  first 1k evaluations) triggers a subtle aurora sweep on the pulse
  pane and a ledger entry marked `milestone: true`.
- The pulse pane's colour temperature tracks `R` — cool when stable,
  warm when the edge-band is active. No sudden jumps; it's always
  readable peripherally.
- CARL's daily observation is written in their voice, not generic copy.
  Small inside jokes seeded from the user's domain tag.

## 9 · Navigation

There is no top-level nav. The dashboard is home. Cmd+K opens the
palette with: **Fit, Compose, Evaluate, Browse, Ledger, Settings,
Help**. Everything is one keystroke away and named the same thing in
the CLI.

## 10 · Visual language

- Dark base, `#0A0A0C`. Opacity-only accents; no secondary hues except
  the pulse colour (aurora gradient).
- xterm + status line aesthetic extended to the web.
- Typography: JetBrains Mono Nerd Font for code, Inter for prose.
- No emojis in product copy. No exclamation points in system messages.
- Motion: every animation uses the same ease curve
  `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1)` and respects
  `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## 11 · Out of scope for v0.9.0

- Team / org accounts. First cohort is individual.
- Mobile app. Web + CLI only.
- Any social layer. No comments, no following. Ratings only.
- Billing dashboards beyond a single "credit balance" line.
- Anything that requires a hosted fitter. Fitting stays local.

## 12 · Acceptance checklist

Summercamp ships when:

- [ ] First-run completes in under 30 s on a 2019 MacBook Pro.
- [ ] Every CLI command in §7 has a UX twin.
- [ ] Edge-band breach message reaches the user within 2 s of the
      Lyapunov proxy leaving the band.
- [ ] Composition UX enforces the depth cap in the browser (not just
      the server) for instant feedback.
- [ ] Marketplace purchase round-trip completes under 3 s on wired
      ethernet (excluding on-chain confirmation).
- [ ] Dashboard scrolls smoothly at 60 fps on integrated GPU.
- [ ] Offline mode (no network) renders the last cached dashboard with
      a "Serving cached" chip — never a blank state.
